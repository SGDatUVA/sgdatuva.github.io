Officers

President - Jimmy Connors

James "Jimmy" Connors is a 4rd year Computer Science major in the School of Engineering. He was a programmer on Into the Depths in Fall of 2019 and Spring of 2020, and also worked as a programmer on Fly in Spring 2021. He enjoys designing gimmick-filled platformer games for game jams in his free time, and his favorite game is Breath of the Wild.

Vice President - Spencer Portuese

Spencer Portuese is a 3rd year double majoring in Computer Science and Archaeology. He directed Stayin’ Alive in Spring 2021 and DashBall in Fall 2021, and is excited for future projects. His favorite game genre is RPGs and enjoys falling into the worlds of the games.

Internal Relations - Vinny Peng

Vinny Peng is a 4th year double majoring in Computer Science and History and minoring in astronomy. He is currently the Internal Relations Officer in charge of communicating important events within the organization. Having been part or and directed multiple games such as Stayin' Alive, Dashball, Astroquest, etc., he has developed a strong passion and interest about player interaction, story telling, and the technical side of games. As someone with both the technical training and creative side of the arts background, he aspires to build immersive virtual worlds for AAA game companies in the future. Favorite game: The Wicther 3: Wild Hunt.

External Relations - Zach Hay

Zach Hay is a second year double majoring in mechanical engineering and computer science. He worked on Astroquest VR last spring and loves working with virtual reality. He also has a background in mobile app development and 3D design. He is the current External Relations Officer and is in charge of outreach to other clubs, alumni, and industry professionals.

Head of Directors - Ian Harvey

Ian Harvey is a 3rd year in the School of Engineering majoring in Computer Science and minoring in both English and business. He is responsible for the masterpiece of a game known only as Atlas during the Fall 2021 semester and is looking forward to working on more club projects in the future. His interests find themselves deeply invested in the extraordinary potentials of game design. Unlimited by the boundaries of conventional media, games to him represent the culmination of all that could ever be made by the human heart. In a sense, they are a means to explore all avenues of art at once, and, by that concept, he views the craft of game development as something capable of discovering every individual artistic strength and weakness that may manifest in the individual. Philosophy aside, Ian enjoys creative writing and playing games in his free time. His favorite game at the moment (the fool that he is) is Destiny 2. On the side, he is writing a book that he hopes to one day finish.
